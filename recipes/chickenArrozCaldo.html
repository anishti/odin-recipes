Chicken Arroz Caldo

Arroz caldo is a lugaw (glutinous rice dish) made with chicken and rice.
It’s flavored with garlic, onions, ginger, fish sauce, and other seasonings.
Though “arroz caldo” is a Spanish name, the dish’s origins are Filipino —
it is an adaptation of a congee that was created by Chinese-Filipino immigrants.

Ingredients

  • olive Oil:2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Onion:1 onion, diced
  • Garlic:2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • Ginger:1 (2 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and thinly sliced
  • Chicken:2 ¼ pounds chicken wings, split and tips discarded
  • Fish Sauce:1 tablespoon fish sauce (Optional)
  • Chicken Broth:5 ¼ cups chicken broth
  • Sweet Rice1 cup glutinous sweet rice

    • Steps

    1. Cook the onion, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.
    2. Add the chicken and stir in the fish sauce.
    3. Pour in the broth and sweet rice, then bring the mixture to a boil.
    4. Season and continue cooking until the chicken is fully cooked.
